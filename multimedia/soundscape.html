Sound Scape

Otago Polytechnic Bachelor of IT - Multimedia Development - Semester 2 2017

Being an avid listener of Trance, Drum and Base, and New Age music, I wanted to combine these genres into an immersive listening experience of being in a rain forest. I sought inspiration from the amazing scenic environment Tomb Rader series had to offer. Specifically the scene when to Lara Croft parachutes through the rainforest while falling through a broken plane window. I wanted to somewhat mimic the suspense of quick time events of a player running from sliding rocks caused by a strong thunder strike in a rainforest.

Sound Scape Timeline

×

To plan the events, I drew up an overview of a sound scape timeline. Also, I wanted to go for the rolling in thunder feel for the sound scape. This meant there was going to be different phases from sunny to calm to which I plotted when each track is going to occur at which stage. This gave me a clear picture of the overall sound scape environment.

Many of the sounds were taken from the Sound Bible. Because it was an attributable website. I had freedom to pick and choose from their wide array of sounds. This included from the howling monkeys, to the rolling in the thunder. However, the freedom of choice came at a price when I simply included too many sounds in soundscape causing it sound like a mess. Also, I included rich sounds from my time at Lake Waihola. That in itself had sounds of the breeze, birds chirping, and the lake water brushing up on the coast.

×

There was so many tracks that I wanted to integrate into the sound scape. To differentiate between them, I had to adjust the left and right decibel slider to create the immersive feeling of being in a rainforest. An example of this is the sound of the approaching thunder storm in one ear, and the rockslide in the other. I searched for some examples of science fiction games and their rainforest scenes to get a feel for the sound story I wanted to create.

×

Using the move tool to move, I reorganized much of the tracks to create the most natural sound scape. Readjustments, was accompanied by blending in the tracks using the envelope tool. I had to use the play-at-speed tool to replay music at a slower pace to fine tune the tracks. There was an issue of the sound output, but this was solved by reducing the amount of playback devices connected to the PC. To create the suspense of running from a rockslide, using the “change temp” I had to speed up the footsteps when the thunder and lightning happened.
